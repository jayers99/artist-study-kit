# Curation UX for Art Study: A Research-Driven Design Framework

This framework defines the systemic architectural requirements and interface affordances necessary to transition from passive digital asset management (DAM) to an active pedagogical environment. As Senior UX Architects, we must design for the "visual library"—a cognitive repository developed through high-velocity triage and deep curatorial sense-making.

---

### 1. Visual Triage and Culling Workflows
The initial stage of curation is "The Cull," where speed and flow-state are paramount. We must minimize the "interaction cost" of categorizing massive candidate pools.

#### Binary vs. Tertiary Selection Models
*   **Tertiary Selection (The Native Standard):** Derived from professional tools like Lightroom and Photo Mechanic, the **P** (Pick), **X** (Reject), and **U** (Unflag) shortcut pattern is the industry standard for categorizing assets into winners, losers, and neutrals.
*   **Left-Hand Ergonomic Optimization:** While P/X/U is standard, native support for left-hand mapping is a critical affordance. As documented in user Reddit discourse, an optimized ergonomic layout (e.g., **F** for Pick, **S** for Reject, **D** for Unflag) allows the right hand to remain on the navigation keys, reducing cognitive and physical friction during thousand-image triage sessions.

#### Systemic Support for Flow-State
*   **Auto-Advance (The "Caps Lock" Pattern):** The interface must support a toggleable state where applying a flag or rating triggers an immediate advance to the next frame. This eliminates redundant navigation clicks and sustains a high-velocity "flow state."
*   **Computational Survey View (ARIES):** Beyond standard galleries, the UI should implement a "Survey View" inspired by ARIES. This view must utilize computational image processing for advanced feature-matching, allowing artists to perform precise side-by-side comparisons of visually similar candidates to identify the most "learnable" reference.

### 2. Choice Architecture: From Weeding to Curating
Our design must facilitate the transition from **Jurying** (the subtractive act of weeding out technical failures) to **Curating** (the additive act of building meaning around a thesis).

#### The Curatorial Process (Alyson Stanfield)
1.  **Stage 1: Jurying (Subtraction):** A high-speed phase where the user "weeds out" work from automated candidate pools. The objective is to remove "non-candidates"—technical errors or irrelevant references.
2.  **Stage 2: Curating (Addition):** The artist starts with a "Curatorial Thesis" (e.g., "The Jagged Line as Emotional Turmoil"). They select 1–2 anchor pieces and build an "ideal selection set" around them.

#### Managing Urgency and Overload
*   **The Urgency Threshold:** Cognitive research suggests that a selection set exceeding 8 to 10 works induces choice paralysis. To maintain focus on an infinite canvas (like refern or PureRef), the UX should include a "Focus Mode" that "mutes" or hides all non-shortlisted assets. This maintains a "sense of urgency" for the current study target, preventing the canvas from becoming a disorganized "visual heap."

Metadata is not just for retrieval; it is for assessing educational value. The system must surface the "Elements of Art" (Trujillo) to help the artist decide if a work is worthy of deep analysis.

### 4. Selection Heuristics for Master Study
The curation system should enforce heuristics that prioritize "Skill-based Selection" (the challenging) over "Taste-based Selection" (the pretty).

*   **The Learnability Ladder (Marc Brunet):**
    *   **Level 1 (Tracing):** Requires UI overlays (refern/ARIES) to develop mechanical muscle memory.
    *   **Level 2 (Simple Study):** Focused repetition of a single element (e.g., Value).
    *   **Level 3 (Applied Reference):** Using a reference to solve a specific problem in a new pose.
    *   **Level 4 (Mental Library):** Synthesis from imagination based on previously curated studies.
*   **"Truth to Materials" Filter:** Selection must favor works that showcase material handling. Educators should pick references like **Rodin’s clay models** for their "energetic imprints" of hands, or **Henry Moore’s *Sheep***, which uses a "vocabulary of swirling scribbles" (hatched/scribbled lines) to empathy-link the line to the bouncy texture of wool.

### 5. Productive Friction: Maintaining Human Intent
Automation often "short-circuits" the learning process by removing the need for active observation. We must implement **Productive Friction** to ensure the artist remains a "geological agent" (Smithson) in their learning.

#### The Curatorial Gate
To prevent the curation of a passive "visual heap," the UI should implement a **"Curatorial Gate."** When a user attempts to move an image from the Library to a Canvas, the system triggers a required "Sense-making" sidebar. This forces the artist to:
1.  Assign at least one "Element of Art" tag.
2.  Input a brief observation (Annotation).
This creates a necessary pause—a moment of **Cognitive Friction** where the artist must "make sense of the art" (Stanfield) before it is deemed part of the shortlist.

### 6. Recommended Curation Workflow & Layout
This 3-step pipeline bridges high-speed DAM with deep-work canvas environments.

1.  **The Gather (Raw Inbox):**
    *   **Layout:** Bulk Grid with "Hover Previews" (Eagle).
    *   **Action:** Rapid ingestion via browser extensions.
2.  **The Cull (Binary Triage):**
    *   **Layout:** Full-screen "Light Box" or "Survey View" (ARIES).
    *   **Action:** S/D/F ergonomic shortcuts with "Auto-advance" engaged to remove technical failures.
3.  **The Shortlist (Thesis-Driven Canvas):**
    *   **Layout:** Infinite Canvas with "Relationship Graphs" (refern/Obsidian).
    *   **Action:** Enforced "Curatorial Gate" to move assets into spatial arrangements, using **relative-size scaling** to compare disparate works (e.g., a building vs. a fighter jet).

### 7. Selection-Support Artifacts & Shortlist Handoff
The goal of the handoff is **"The Art of Unseeing" (Lane Brown)**—transforming a "pretty picture" into a "deconstructed diagram."

#### Shortlist Rationale Template
For every work that passes the Curatorial Gate, the following metadata must be finalized:
*   **The Curatorial Thesis:** (e.g., "Analyzing how Picasso uses jagged lines to suggest turmoil in *Weeping Woman*.")
*   **Visual Anchor:** (Identify the focus: Line, Value, or Texture.)
*   **Handoff Note:** (Comparative prompt: "Contrast the line economy of this Rembrandt sketch with the structural power of Delaunay's Eiffel Tower.")

#### Technical Handoff Tools
The handoff to the deep-analysis environment must include **9-patch overlay tools**. Based on user-led design from PureRef forums, these stretchable arrows, circles, and rounded-rectangle frames allow artists to draw non-destructive diagrams over the reference. This functionality supports the "unseeing" process, allowing the artist to see through the subject to the underlying structural relationships.
