# Research prompt — Spaced repetition and retention for visual and procedural skill

I'm building a tool to help an artist **durably internalize** a master's "visual grammar" (style traits, value structure, palette logic, mark-making) and their own technique over time. I want a practical **retention and review system**, grounded in research, that goes beyond one-off studies.

Cover the science and prior art:
- **Spaced repetition** and the **spacing/lag effect**; scheduling algorithms and tools (**Leitner system**, **Anki**, **SuperMemo/SM-2**, FSRS) — how they work and their evidence base.
- **Interleaving** of skills/subjects and its effect on discrimination and transfer.
- **Retrieval practice for visual material** — what's known about flashcards and testing for images, perceptual categories, and concepts (vs. purely verbal facts).
- **Deliberate practice** and **motor/procedural skill** retention — how spacing and feedback apply to a physical skill like drawing/painting, and where SRS does and doesn't transfer.
- **Perceptual learning** — training the eye to discriminate style features, edges, value relationships.

Conclude with: (a) a **practical review system** for an artist internalizing a specific artist's style — what to review, in what format, on what schedule; (b) how to mix **declarative** review (facts, vocabulary, "what to notice") with **perceptual** and **procedural** practice (re-doing study passages, comparison drills); (c) concrete **artifacts** the tool could generate (e.g. spaced study-passage drills, style-discrimination card sets, scheduled re-studies); and (d) honest **limits** of SRS for skill acquisition. Cite research and reputable sources.
