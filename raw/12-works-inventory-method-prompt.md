# Research prompt — Method for selecting and ranking an artist's most important works

I'm building a tool that, given a **historical artist**, produces an **inventory of their
most important works** — a principled "canon selection" for a studio artist preparing
master studies. I need a research-grounded **method** for identifying, ranking, and
clustering the key works, and for capturing the right metadata per work.

Cover:

- **What makes a work "important"** — art-historical significance, technical innovation,
  representativeness of the artist's style, fame/iconicity, influence on others, scholarly
  and exhibition attention. How to weigh these signals.
- **Authoritative sources for canon** — catalogues raisonnés, museum collection catalogues,
  major surveys and monographs, exhibition history, and how to use them; handling
  **attribution and authenticity** (workshop, school-of, contested works).
- **Important vs. studyable** — distinguish *art-historically important* from *best for a
  master study* (clear technique, good reference images, instructive at the learner's level).
  A work can be canonical yet poor to study, and vice versa.
- **Ranking and clustering** — how to order or group works (by period, by technique/problem,
  by difficulty, by teaching value) so a learner gets a sensible study sequence.
- **Per-work metadata schema** — title, date, medium, dimensions, current collection,
  *why it matters*, relation to the artist's development, image availability/quality.

Conclude with: (a) a **selection rubric** (weighted criteria) for ranking works; (b) a
**per-work metadata schema**; and (c) a method to produce a **ranked, clustered inventory**
that doubles as a study sequence. Cite reputable art-historical sources and reference works.
