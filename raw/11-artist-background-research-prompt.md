# Research prompt — Method for researching an artist's biography and art-historical context

I'm building a tool that, given a **historical artist's name**, produces a concise
**background report** for a *studio artist* preparing a master study — not for an academic.
I need a research-grounded **method** for biographical synthesis and art-historical
placement: what to gather, from where, and how to structure it so it actually informs
*why and how* the artist painted the way they did.

Cover:

- **Biographical synthesis** — the life facts that matter for studio context: training and
  teachers, geography and workshops, career periods/phases, materials and working methods,
  patrons and commissions. Distinguish signal from trivia.
- **Art-historical placement** — situating the artist among movements, influences, peers,
  and followers; their innovations and significance; how scholars periodize their output.
- **Canonical reference sources and how to use them** — Grove Art / Oxford Art Online,
  catalogues raisonnés, museum scholarly pages, monographs and exhibition catalogues,
  Smarthistory / Khan Academy, university art-history resources. How to triage and cite.
- **Biography → style bridge** — which biographical facts actually predict visual choices
  (e.g., a teacher's palette, a workshop's methods, a period's constraints), so the
  background feeds the style/visual-analysis stages rather than sitting inert.
- **Anti-slop** — avoiding SEO filler, poster-shop bios, and shallow aggregation.

Conclude with: (a) a **report structure / template** for the background section (sections,
length, what each must answer); (b) a **source-priority order** for biographical research;
and (c) a checklist of **what to extract that connects biography to technique and style**.
Cite reputable sources and art-historical reference works.
