# LLM Creativity vs. Human Cognition: A Benchmarking Analysis

## Executive Summary

Recent large-scale comparative studies indicate that state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs) have reached and, in specific instances, surpassed average human performance on established creativity benchmarks. Research utilizing a dataset of 100,000 human participants reveals that while models like GPT-4 and Gemini Pro can exceed population-average scores on the Divergent Association Task (DAT), they consistently fail to surpass the creative ceiling defined by the top 10% to 50% of human performers. 

Key findings suggest that LLM creativity is highly sensitive to hyperparameter tuning—specifically temperature and sampling methods like "min-p"—and targeted prompt strategies. While GenAI models demonstrate superior performance in both divergent thinking (generating novel ideas) and convergent thinking (identifying correct associations), the validity of traditional psychometric tests like the Remote Associates Test (RAT) is increasingly questioned due to potential data contamination. Ultimately, current evidence suggests that while AI can replace "average" creative output, elite human creative labor remains distinct and superior in narrative complexity and semantic depth.

## I. Comparative Divergent Thinking Performance

Divergent thinking is defined as the ability to generate novel and diverse solutions to open-ended problems. It is a core index of creative cognition, typically measured through associative distance and ideational flexibility.

### The Divergent Association Task (DAT)
The DAT requires participants to produce 10 words that are as semantically distant as possible. Performance is scored using objective semantic-distance metrics.

*   **Human-AI Benchmarking:** In a study involving 100,000 humans, GPT-4 significantly outperformed the human mean. Gemini Pro performed at a level statistically indistinguishable from the human average.
*   **The Creative Ceiling:** Despite surpassing the average, even the highest-performing LLMs remain below the mean scores of the top 50% of human participants. The top human decile and quartile maintain a clear performance gap over all tested models, including GPT-4 and Claude 3.
*   **Model Variability:** Performance is not strictly tied to model size. For example, Vicuna (a smaller model) outperformed several larger counterparts. Notably, GPT-4-turbo showed a decline in performance compared to the original GPT-4, exhibiting higher word repetition (e.g., the word "ocean" appeared in 90% of its response sets).

### The Alternate Uses Task (AUT)
The AUT measures the ability to generate unconventional uses for common objects (e.g., a "tire" or "shoe").

*   **Originality Scores:** GenAI models (ChatGPT-4o, DeepSeek-V3, and Gemini 2.0) consistently outperform student samples in both "average" originality (median) and "best" idea originality (maximum).
*   **Cognitive Constraints:** Researchers suggest humans are limited by functional fixedness and social norms, whereas LLMs freely recombine concepts from vast training data.
*   **Model Comparison:** ChatGPT-4o consistently demonstrates the highest originality scores, followed by Gemini 2.0 and DeepSeek-V3.

| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Elite** | Top 10% Humans | Maintain the highest semantic distance in DAT. |
| **Advanced** | GPT-4 / ChatGPT-4o | Surpasses human average; highest among AI. |
| **Average** | Gemini Pro / Human Mean | Statistically similar performance on DAT. |
| **Baseline** | Student Samples | Surpassed by state-of-the-art GenAI on AUT/RAT. |

## II. Convergent Thinking and Logical Association

Convergent thinking involves identifying a single correct solution to a problem by connecting disparate thoughts.

### Remote Associates Test (RAT) Findings
The RAT presents three words (e.g., "spot/throat/loser") and requires a fourth linking word ("sore").

*   **AI Dominance:** All tested GenAI models demonstrated superior performance compared to humans. ChatGPT-4o achieved near-perfect accuracy (mean 54.96 out of 57 items).
*   **Contamination Concerns:** The exceptionally high accuracy and restricted variance in AI scores suggest a "ceiling effect." Because RAT answers are widely available online, these tests may now be inappropriate for assessing genuine AI creativity, as results likely reflect data retrieval rather than reasoning.

## III. Creative Writing and Narrative Integration

To evaluate "Divergent Semantic Integration" (DSI), research expanded beyond single-word tasks to longer-form creative writing, including haikus, movie synopses, and flash fiction.

### Semantic Diversity in Narratives
*   **Metric Accuracy:** DSI, which uses BERT to measure semantic distance between successive sentences, correlates strongly with human perceptions of creativity.
*   **Performance Trends:** GPT-4 consistently outperforms GPT-3.5 across all formats. However, human-written haikus and synopses maintain a significant edge in semantic divergence over all LLMs.
*   **Stylistic Homogeneity:** LLMs tend to occupy distinct regions in embedding space, suggesting specific stylistic patterns. Human writing exhibits broader thematic variance and higher Lempel-Ziv (LZ) complexity in poetry, often because humans adhere less strictly to formal constraints (e.g., nature imagery in haikus) than models do.

## IV. Optimization: Prompting, Temperature, and Sampling

LLM creative output is not static; it can be significantly modulated through technical adjustments.

### Prompt Strategies
Explicitly instructing an LLM to use specific linguistic strategies can enhance its semantic divergence.
*   **Effective Strategies:** Prompts focusing on "varying etymology" or using a "thesaurus" yielded higher DAT scores than the basic instructions.
*   **Ineffective Strategies:** Strategies based on "meaning opposition" (antonyms) decreased scores because opposing words are often semantically related in latent space.

### Hyperparameter Tuning: Temperature and Min-p
*   **Temperature:** Increasing temperature (e.g., from 0.5 to 1.5) reduces deterministic responses, fostering exploration. In GPT-4, higher temperatures led to a significant rise in DAT scores and narrative DSI, though it can occasionally reduce coherence in shorter formats like haikus.
*   **Min-p Sampling:** This dynamic truncation method adjusts the sampling threshold based on the top token's probability. Unlike "top-p" (nucleus sampling), min-p improves text quality and diversity at high temperatures, making it a superior choice for creative writing tasks. It has been shown to improve scores on creative benchmarks like AlpacaEval.

## V. Critical Analysis of AI "Creativity"

The debate persists regarding whether LLM outputs constitute genuine creativity or statistical recombination.

*   **Associative vs. Inventive Thought:** AI creativity is currently viewed as a "variation" phase (exploration) without an autonomous "selection" phase (evaluation based on personal experience or intent).
*   **Quality vs. Novelty:** While LLMs excel at novelty (semantic distance), they may lag in "appropriateness" or "usefulness." Some studies suggest that as models become more "aligned" (safe and appropriate), their creative novelty may decrease.
*   **The Human-AI Synergy:** Current research suggests that AI is best utilized as a collaborative partner. While AI increases individual productivity and creative self-efficacy, it may inadvertently homogenize collective output, narrowing the diversity of ideas across the broader population.

> "The persistent gap between the best-performing humans and even the most advanced LLMs indicates that the most demanding creative roles in industry are unlikely to be supplanted by current artificial intelligence systems." — *Scientific Reports, 2026*
