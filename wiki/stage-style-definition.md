# Style Definition

> [!info] Pipeline role
> Before any single work is studied, articulate what makes the artist *recognizable* — the
> formal, technical, and compositional traits that separate them from others. Emits the
> "visual grammar" section of `report.md` and a reusable style cheat sheet.

## What the research says

This stage applies the [[concept-formal-analysis-taxonomy]] (from
[[04.1-style-analysis-frameworks]]) at the level of the *artist*, not a single work: their
characteristic Notan/value structure, palette logic (e.g. limited vs full spectrum),
form-construction sequence, and surface handling (glazing vs scumbling).

The taxonomy supplies the **precise vocabulary** — opposition/subordination, chroma/
temperature, broken film — that keeps the description diagnostic rather than vague. It also
maps cleanly onto the seed's style dimensions (line, shape, value, mark-making, etc.).

The biography→style signals from [[stage-background-research]] feed in here: workshop
methods, material constraints, and handedness explain *why* the grammar looks as it does.

## Open questions / tensions

- Style varies across an artist's career — does this stage produce one grammar or a per-
  period set? (Likely: a top-level grammar + period deltas, sequenced by
  [[stage-works-inventory]].)
- Grammar should be grounded in actual images, but image acquisition is
  [[stage-image-discovery]] (later). Resolve by drafting from sources now, refining after
  images land.

## Skill design implications

- Emit a **style cheat sheet** (a [[concept-worked-example-fading]] aid): chunked
  "structural DNA" across the taxonomy's four levels, each chunk in the artist's terms.
- Express grammar as testable traits ("warm high-chroma lights, muted cool shadows";
  "edges lost on the shadow side") so [[stage-visual-analysis]] can confirm/deny per work
  and [[stage-study-retention]] can turn them into discrimination cards.
- Keep the artist-grammar note distinct from per-work analysis; link the two.
- Note period variation explicitly when the sources support it.
