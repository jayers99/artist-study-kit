# Spaced Repetition & Retention

How to make a master's "visual grammar" durable. Source:
[[08.1-spaced-repetition-retention]]. Used by [[stage-study-retention]] (the review system)
and referenced by [[stage-visual-analysis]] (memory-based reconstruction). Pairs with
[[concept-desirable-difficulty]].

## Spacing over massing

~70% of new information is lost within 24h without review. The **spacing effect** + **lag
effect** (a U-curve: moderate gaps beat cramming; too-long gaps force a relearn) set the
schedule.

## Scheduling algorithms

Leitner (coarse) → SM-2 (fixed ease factors) → **FSRS** (Difficulty/Stability/
Retrievability model; ~20–30% more efficient than SM-2). Use FSRS for declarative facts.

## Two learning systems (COVIS)

- **Rule-based (RB):** prefrontal/working memory; declarative — color theory, perspective.
- **Information-integration (II):** striatum/dopamine; perceptual/procedural — visual
  grammar, edges, "style." Learned slowly, non-verbalizable, feedback-dependent.

## Perceptual & procedural levers

- **500ms feedback rule:** procedural reinforcement is optimal ~500ms after the stimulus
  (aligns dopamine with CaMKII); degrades by 1s, halts by 2.5s.
- **Retrieval practice:** reconstruct from memory (not copy-while-looking) → +50–80% recall.
- **Interleaving:** mix styles (Baroque lighting ↔ Impressionist edges) → 2× discrimination.
- **Woodpecker cycles:** solve a 50–100-item style-artifact set repeatedly over a week,
  dropping solve-time while holding accuracy.

## Honest limits

SRS can't model real-time brush-on-canvas tactile feedback ("enacted retrieval"); passive
review breeds the fluency illusion; **active production** (drawing from memory) beats
on-screen recognition.
