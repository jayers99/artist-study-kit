# Background Research

> [!info] Pipeline role
> Given an artist name, produce a concise background report for a *studio* artist — and,
> crucially, surface the biographical facts that *predict* the visual choices the later
> stages will analyze. Emits `report.md` (background section).

## What the research says

[[11.1-artist-background-research]] argues for an **"archaeological" / technical
art-history** stance over aestheticized biography: treat the work as an artifact and read
it bottom-up. The high-value move for studio prep is the **biography → style bridge** —
specific life facts translate into technical choices:

- **Lineage/workshop training** sets foundational methods (inherited techniques).
- **Geography & materials** are recorded in the work (paper stocks, watermarks date pieces).
- **Political/economic pressure** shifts genre and method (e.g. Reformation → efficient
  secular portraiture).
- **Legal status** can imply autograph vs workshop production.
- **Physical traits** (e.g. handedness) are diagnostic of stroke direction.

Source triage prioritizes **technical theses & conservation records** and **primary
objects/inventories** over 19th–20th-c. aesthetic critique (least reliable). This dovetails
with [[concept-source-trust-signals]] for grading the web sources behind the bio.

## Open questions / tensions

- The research skews to a deeply-documented Old Master (Holbein); for many artists the
  technical-conservation record is thin. The method must **degrade gracefully** to
  museum essays + monographs when conservation data doesn't exist.
- "Signal vs trivia" is judgment-heavy — what's the heuristic the skill applies?
- Tension with anti-slop: the most accessible web bios are often the least reliable.

## Skill design implications

- Emit a background section answering: training/lineage, geography & movement, periods,
  patrons/economics, art-historical placement, **why they matter**, and a curated
  resources list (graded via [[stage-source-grading]]).
- Run an explicit **biography→style checklist** (workshop continuity, material dating,
  legal/autograph status, handedness, functional design) so the output *hands signal* to
  [[stage-style-definition]] rather than sitting inert.
- Apply [[concept-source-trust-signals]] as a filter; prefer technical/primary sources,
  flag aesthetic-only critique as low-confidence.
- Provide a **report template** (substrate → media sequencing → function → relation to
  paintings) as the rich-data path, with a lighter fallback for thinly-documented artists.
